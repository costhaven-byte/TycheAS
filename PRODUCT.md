# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Owners and managers of local service businesses — HVAC, mechanics, catering,
clinics, salons, contractors, restaurants, real estate, home services. They're
busy, hands-on, often reading on a phone between jobs, and skeptical of "AI"
hype. They don't care about technology; they care about not losing money to
missed leads, slow replies, and slow quotes.

## Product Purpose

Lucrator is a CRM for local service businesses. The core product is the system
of record for leads and customers: a live pipeline, hot/warm/cold scoring, status
tracking, and an analytics dashboard — so no lead slips through. Optional growth
modules bolt straight onto the pipeline: a 24/7 chatbot (which now also acts as a
booking & buying agent — it drops appointments onto the calendar and logs sales to
the pipeline as part of the same chatbot package), a follow-up & reviews
engine (with direct links to Google, Facebook, and others), a quote assistant, an
appointment manager, and a landing page. The Growth Engine bundle packages every
module together with 24/7 priority support, increased chatbot limits, and a
done-for-you custom CRM setup — sold as the best-value, most-bought option. The
site's job is to make a skeptical owner think "this is real, this would make me
money" and book a free audit.

## Brand Personality

Grounded, precise, prosperous. The voice of a sharp operator who closes deals —
confident and direct, never futuristic or buzzwordy. The name is Lucrator (Latin
*lucrari*, "to gain/profit"): the through-line is leads turned into revenue —
"turn leads into revenue." Trust is earned by sounding believable, not by
promising 10x overnight.

## Anti-references

- The saturated AI-agency look: near-black background, indigo→cyan gradient,
  gradient headline text, gradient buttons, glassmorphism, a tracked-uppercase
  eyebrow above every section, endless identical icon cards. (This is exactly
  what the first build looked like; the renovation exists to escape it.)
- Generic SaaS template energy. Must feel like a real, specific business.
- Editorial-magazine cosplay (display-serif + italic + drop caps). Not the lane.

## Design Principles

1. **Sell the outcome, not the tech.** Lead with lost-money problems and won
   business. "AI" is named only where it's literally the mechanism.
2. **Believable over hyped.** Concrete copy, no exaggerated claims.
3. **Operator, not demo.** Looks like a business that closes, not an AI showcase.
4. **One confident color, used decisively.** Committed clay accent on warm-neutral
   off-white + near-black ink. No gradients carrying meaning.
5. **Earn every section's motion.** Reveals fit what they reveal; reduced-motion
   is a real alternative.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1. Full keyboard operability,
visible focus rings, honest `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives, mobile-first.
